Adverse Event Term(s):
Acute liver injury, nausea, jaundice, abdominal pain

Brief description of participant with no personal identifiers:
A 46-year-old female participant enrolled in the rheumatoid arthritis trial presented with progressive nausea, fatigue, right upper abdominal pain, and yellow discoloration of eyes approximately 5 days after initiation of investigational drug AXM-201.

Brief description of the nature of the serious adverse event:
The participant reported loss of appetite and dark urine followed by worsening abdominal discomfort. Liver function tests showed marked elevation of ALT and AST. She was admitted for monitoring and supportive care. No prior history of chronic liver disease was documented. Study medication was discontinued immediately upon suspicion of drug-induced liver injury. Follow-up review documented biochemical improvement before discharge.
